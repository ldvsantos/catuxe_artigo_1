\noindent
On behalf of all co-authors, I am pleased to submit the manuscript entitled ``Machine Learning Readiness to Support Agroecological Transitions in Traditional Agricultural Systems'' for consideration in the \textit{Journal of Agricultural, Biological and Environmental Statistics}.

\vspace{6pt}

\noindent
This work presents the first comprehensive meta-analysis assessing the operational readiness of machine learning to support evidence-based monitoring and agroecological transitions in Traditional Agricultural Systems (TAS). Analyzing 244 studies (2010--2025) through random-effects modeling, bibliometric network analysis, Multiple Correspondence Analysis, and FAIR compliance assessment, we quantify the gap between reported algorithmic performance and actual deployment readiness for regulatory and heritage-management applications. Key findings include: (i)~pooled classification accuracy of 90.7\% (95\% CI 89.8--91.5\%) with substantial heterogeneity ($I^{2} = 58\%$); (ii)~deep learning adoption rising from 4.8\% to 21.1\% between 2015--2019 and 2020--2025; (iii)~critically low FAIR compliance (mean 18.7/100), with only 1.1\% of studies sharing code or data; and (iv)~publication-bias diagnostics (Egger's test, $p = 0.009$; \textit{trim-and-fill} correction of $-$1.54~pp) indicating systematic overestimation.

\vspace{6pt}

\noindent
We believe this manuscript aligns closely with the scope of the \textit{Journal of Agricultural, Biological and Environmental Statistics}. The study integrates random-effects meta-analysis, publication-bias diagnostics (Egger's test, trim-and-fill), co-occurrence network analysis (modularity optimization), and Multiple Correspondence Analysis with hierarchical clustering to characterize 244 studies on machine learning applied to Traditional Agricultural Systems. These statistical and multivariate methods are applied to agricultural, biological, and environmental data, directly addressing the journal's core interest in innovative statistical methodology for scientific investigation in these domains. The meta-analytic framework quantifies heterogeneity ($I^2 = 58\%$), detects reporting asymmetry ($p = 0.009$), and provides bias-adjusted pooled estimates, while the multivariate analyses reveal latent thematic structures and functional groupings in a high-dimensional categorical corpus. A FAIR data-governance assessment further examines reproducibility and open-science compliance across the field. By combining advanced statistical synthesis with applied agricultural and environmental questions, the manuscript contributes to the interdisciplinary mission of JABES at the intersection of statistical science and agricultural, biological, and environmental research.
